# Chisholm FC League Rules & Regulations

*Version 1.0 - Draft*

These rules govern the Chisholm FC League. Where not explicitly stated, FIFA Laws of the Game and US Soccer Federation guidelines apply.

---

## Part I: FIFA Alignment

### Article 1: Governing Standards

1.1 The Laws of the Game as published by the International Football Association Board (IFAB) and FIFA shall apply to all matches.

1.2 US Soccer Federation rules supersede local rules where applicable for player eligibility and disciplinary matters.

1.3 This document supplements but does not replace FIFA Laws of the Game.

### Article 2: Pathway to Federation

The Chisholm FC League operates with eventual US Soccer Adult Member League affiliation as a goal. This means:

| Requirement | Status | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| US Soccer Adult League Registration | Planned Year 2 | Required for inter-league play |
| USSF Referee Certification | Required | All center refs must be Grade 8+ |
| Player Registration with US Soccer | Planned | Enables national competition pathway |
| Insurance via USSF | Planned | Better coverage, federation recognition |
| SafeSport Compliance | Required | All coaches and admins |

### Article 3: Pathway Integration

3.1 **Upward Pathway**: Players demonstrating exceptional ability may be recommended to:
- USL League Two tryouts
- MLS NEXT Pro open tryouts
- NWSL/USL W League opportunities
- US Soccer regional camps (Open Cup qualification)

3.2 **US Open Cup Qualification**: As an adult amateur league, clubs may eventually be eligible for US Open Cup qualifying rounds through proper US Soccer registration.

3.3 **Referee Development**: Referees can earn higher USSF grades through league experience.

## Part II: Competition Rules

### Article 4: League Structure

4.1 **Divisions**
- Men's Premier Division (competitive)
- Men's Open Division (developmental)
- Women's Premier Division (competitive)
- Women's Open Division (developmental)

4.2 **Club Requirements**
- Each club MUST field at least one (1) Men's team and one (1) Women's team
- Clubs MAY field additional teams in Open divisions
- All teams within a club share one Club registration

4.3 **Combined Gender Championship**
- Club standings combine Men's and Women's results
- The Club Championship is awarded based on combined points
- Individual division champions are also recognized

### Article 5: Points System

5.1 **Match Points** (per FIFA standard)
| Result | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Win | 3 |
| Draw | 1 |
| Loss | 0 |

5.2 **Club Points**
```
Club Points = Men's Team Points + Women's Team Points
```

5.3 **Tiebreaker Procedure** (in order)
1. Head-to-head Club record (combined results in meetings)
2. Combined goal difference
3. Combined goals scored
4. Disciplinary record (fewer red/yellow card points)
5. Drawing of lots

### Article 6: Match Duration

6.1 **Playing Time** (per FIFA Laws)
- Two (2) periods of forty-five (45) minutes
- Halftime interval of fifteen (15) minutes
- Referee may add time for stoppages (injury time)

6.2 **Regular Season**: No extra time. Draws stand.

6.3 **Playoffs/Championship**:
- Two (2) periods of fifteen (15) minutes extra time
- If still level, penalty kicks per FIFA Laws

### Article 7: Ball and Equipment

7.1 **The Ball**
- Size 5 match ball conforming to FIFA standards
- Home team provides match ball
- Referee has final approval

7.2 **Player Equipment** (per FIFA Law 4)
- Jersey with number (unique within team)
- Shorts
- Socks (team color, covering shin guards)
- Shin guards (mandatory)
- Footwear (no metal studs; turf or molded cleats)

7.3 **Team Colors**
- Home team wears primary colors
- Away team must bring alternate if colors clash
- Goalkeeper must be distinguishable from both teams and referee

### Article 8: Substitutions

8.1 **Substitution Rules**
- Unlimited substitutions (recreational adult standard)
- Re-entry permitted
- Substitutions at referee's signal only
- Player must exit at nearest boundary
- Substitute enters at midfield

8.2 **Substitution Opportunities**
- Goal kick (either team)
- Throw-in (team with possession)
- After a goal (either team)
- At halftime
- Injury stoppage (only injured player and one opponent)

### Article 9: Minimum Players

9.1 A team must have at least seven (7) players to start or continue a match.

9.2 **Grace Period**: Ten (10) minute grace period before forfeit is declared.

9.3 If a team falls below 7 players during a match, the match is abandoned and recorded as a forfeit (3-0).

## Part III: Player Eligibility

### Article 10: Registration

10.1 All players must be registered with the league before participating.

10.2 **Age Requirement**: Minimum 18 years of age.

10.3 **Documentation Required**:
- Valid government-issued photo ID
- Signed liability waiver
- Emergency contact information
- Medical disclosure form

10.4 Players may only be registered with ONE (1) club per season.

10.5 Players may play for multiple teams within their registered club.

### Article 11: Guest Players

11.1 Guest players from other registered clubs are NOT permitted.

11.2 **Exception**: During playoffs, a club may call up players from their Open division teams to Premier squads if:
- Player was registered with club all season
- Player has appeared in at least 3 matches for their original team
- Call-up is registered 24 hours before match

### Article 12: Transfers

12.1 **Transfer Window**: Opens registration day, closes after Week 4.

12.2 **Mid-Season Transfer**: Requires approval from:
- Releasing club
- Receiving club
- League administration

12.3 Players may only transfer once per season.

12.4 **Transfer Sit-Out**: Players transferring mid-season must sit out one (1) match.

## Part IV: Discipline

### Article 13: Cautions (Yellow Cards)

13.1 Yellow cards are issued per FIFA Laws for cautionable offenses.

13.2 **Accumulation**
- Five (5) yellow cards = automatic one (1) match suspension
- Counter resets after serving suspension
- Cards do not carry over between seasons

13.3 Yellow card offenses include (not exhaustive):
- Unsporting behavior
- Dissent by word or action
- Persistent offenses
- Delaying restart of play
- Failure to respect required distance
- Entering/leaving without permission
- Deliberate handball (not denying goal/obvious opportunity)

### Article 14: Sending Off (Red Cards)

14.1 **Automatic Suspensions**
| Offense | Minimum Suspension |
|---------|-------------------|
| Second yellow card | 1 match |
| Denying obvious goal-scoring opportunity (DOGSO) | 1 match |
| Serious foul play | 2 matches |
| Violent conduct | 3 matches + committee review |
| Spitting at person | 3 matches + committee review |
| Offensive/abusive language or gestures | 2 matches |
| Referee abuse (verbal) | 3 matches |
| Referee abuse (physical) | Season ban + committee review |

14.2 **Immediate Ejection**: Player must leave field area within 2 minutes.

14.3 **Cooling Off Period**: Player may not return to sideline area for remainder of match.

### Article 15: Team Discipline

15.1 **Match Abandonment by Team**:
- 3-0 forfeit recorded
- $200 fine to club
- Club must appear before disciplinary committee

15.2 **Mass Confrontation** (5+ players involved):
- Match abandoned
- All participants receive automatic 1 match suspension
- Committee review required before next match

15.3 **Fan/Spectator Misconduct**:
- Club is responsible for their supporters
- Repeated incidents may result in club sanctions

### Article 16: Appeals

16.1 Appeals must be submitted in writing within 48 hours.

16.2 Appeals require $50 fee (refunded if appeal successful).

16.3 Disciplinary Committee consists of:
- League Commissioner
- Two (2) neutral club representatives
- USSF-certified referee (non-voting advisor)

16.4 Committee decision is final.

## Part V: Match Operations

### Article 17: Scheduling

17.1 League publishes complete season schedule before first match day.

17.2 **Match Day Format**
```
5:00 PM - Women's Match
7:00 PM - Men's Match
```

17.3 Home club is responsible for:
- Field setup and goals
- Corner flags
- Match balls (minimum 3)
- First aid kit
- Water for players and referees

### Article 18: Referees

18.1 All center referees must hold USSF Grade 8 or higher certification.

18.2 Assistant referees (lines) must be USSF Grade 9 or higher.

18.3 Referee payment is handled by the league, not individual clubs.

18.4 **Referee Assignment Priority**
1. Higher-graded referees for Premier matches
2. Developing referees assigned to Open division
3. No referee may officiate their own club's matches

### Article 19: Match Reporting

19.1 **Pre-Match**
- Team rosters submitted 30 minutes before kickoff
- Players not on roster may not participate

19.2 **Post-Match**
- Referee submits match report within 24 hours
- Includes: score, scorers, cards, incidents
- Both captains sign match card confirming score

19.3 **Incident Reports**
- Required for any red card, injury, or misconduct
- Submitted within 24 hours
- Triggers committee review if warranted

### Article 20: Weather & Postponements

20.1 **Field Conditions**
- Referee has final say on playability
- Lightning within 8 miles = mandatory 30-minute delay
- Second lightning within 8 miles = match postponed

20.2 **Extreme Heat**
- Heat index > 105°F: match postponed
- Heat index 100-105°F: mandatory water break each half

20.3 **Rescheduling**
- League coordinates makeup dates
- Match must be completed within 21 days
- If not feasible, league determines resolution

## Part VI: Forfeits

### Article 21: Forfeit Conditions

21.1 A match is declared a forfeit when:
- Team has fewer than 7 players after grace period
- Team accumulates 3+ red cards in a match
- Team refuses to continue play
- Team fields an ineligible player

21.2 **Forfeit Score**: 3-0 awarded to non-forfeiting team.

21.3 **Multiple Forfeits**
| Forfeits | Consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| 2nd forfeit | $100 fine |
| 3rd forfeit | $200 fine + probation |
| 4th forfeit | Removal from league |

## Part VII: Field Requirements

### Article 22: Field Dimensions

22.1 Fields must conform to FIFA standards:
- Length: 100-110 meters (110-120 yards)
- Width: 64-75 meters (70-80 yards)
- Minimum adult: 90m x 45m (100 x 50 yards)

22.2 **Markings**: All required markings per FIFA Laws

22.3 **Goals**: 8 feet x 24 feet (2.44m x 7.32m)

### Article 23: Field Safety

23.1 Referee inspects field before each match.

23.2 Unsafe conditions include:
- Standing water
- Dangerous holes or debris
- Damaged goals (not anchored, broken nets)
- Lightning or severe weather

23.3 Home club must provide safe playing environment.

---

## Part VIII: Protests

### Article 24: Protest Procedure

24.1 Protests must be filed within 24 hours of match conclusion.

24.2 **Valid Protest Grounds**:
- Ineligible player participated
- Rule misapplication affecting outcome
- External interference (not referee judgment)

24.3 **NOT Valid Protest Grounds**:
- Referee judgment calls (offside, fouls, etc.)
- Weather conditions
- Field conditions (if match was started)

24.4 Protest requires $100 fee (refunded if upheld).

24.5 Committee decision is final.

---

## Part IX: Club Operations

### Article 25: Club Responsibilities

25.1 Each club must designate:
- Club Administrator (primary contact)
- Men's Team Manager
- Women's Team Manager
- Treasurer/Financial Contact

25.2 Clubs are responsible for:
- Player registration and fees
- Match day operations (when hosting)
- Uniform provision
- Player conduct (on and off field)
- Spectator behavior

### Article 26: Code of Conduct

26.1 All participants (players, coaches, staff, spectators) must:
- Respect opponents, referees, and officials
- Refrain from discriminatory conduct
- Avoid threatening or violent behavior
- Follow referee instructions
- Accept match results

26.2 Violations may result in sanctions to individual and/or club.

---

## Part X: Amendments

### Article 27: Rule Changes

27.1 Rules may be amended by:
- League Board vote (2/3 majority)
- Emergency provision by Commissioner (temporary)

27.2 Clubs receive 14 days notice of rule changes.

27.3 Mid-season changes are avoided unless urgent.
